**Paper:** TAPIP3D: Tracking Any Point in Persistent 3D Geometry, NeurIPS 2025, TAPIP3D
**Summary:** TAPIP3D does not produce a point cloud or dense 3D scene reconstruction as its final output; it is a feed-forward 3D point tracking model. It takes RGB-D video, or RGB video paired with sensed/estimated depth and camera calibration, lifts image features into 3D feature clouds, and predicts query-point 3D trajectories plus visibility in either camera-centric coordinates or a first-camera/world coordinate frame when poses are available.

1. **3D reconstruction:** No. TAPIP3D outputs 3D trajectories for queried points and a visibility trajectory, not a dense reconstructed point cloud or complete scene representation; its feature clouds are internal representations built from input RGB-D/depth and image features (Sec. 3 overview, pp. 3-4; Sec. 3.2, p. 5). Figure 1 shows point clouds from depth and camera poses provided or estimated by MegaSaM, but those are external geometry inputs rather than TAPIP3D reconstruction outputs (Fig. 1, pp. 1-2).

2. **Dense reconstruction:** No. The method has no dense reconstruction output mode: it tracks query points, reports inference for 1,024 query points over 32 frames, and obtains dense per-frame geometry from supplied or estimated depth maps rather than reconstructing it as an output (Sec. 3, p. 3; Implementation Details, p. 6; Supp. C, p. 14). Although its internal feature maps are dense over image cells, the delivered result is query-based point tracking, not dense scene reconstruction.

3. **Reference frame:** Fixed world. TAPIP3D offers two hardcoded modes — a first-camera world frame (when poses are supplied) and a per-frame camera frame (pose-free mode) — but neither reference is freely designatable by the user; the mode is selected at run time, not the specific reference frame.

4. **Metric scale:** Partial. TAPIP3D preserves the scale of the input geometry rather than independently recovering metric scale: sensor or ground-truth depth can yield metric tracks, and the stereo demonstration says FoundationStereo can produce metric-scaled point tracks, while monocular estimated geometry determines the scale quality (Sec. 3, p. 3; Supp. D, pp. 14-15). The method also normalizes coordinates by a scale factor during processing, so metric scale is inherited from the provided depth/calibration source (Implementation Details, p. 6; Supp. B, p. 14).

5. **Point tracking:** Yes. Point tracking is the paper's central task: for each query point, TAPIP3D predicts a trajectory across frames, establishing temporal correspondences (Sec. 3 overview, p. 3). Its 3D neighborhood module and iterative transformer are designed to refine those query-point tracks over time (Sec. 3.2, pp. 5-6).

6. **3D tracks:** Yes. TAPIP3D explicitly outputs 3D point trajectories, defined as `tau_q = (X, Y, Z)` over the video, for each query point (Sec. 3 overview, p. 3). The experiments evaluate long-term 3D point tracking with AJ3D, APD3D, and OA metrics across TAPVid-3D, LSFOdyssey, DynamicReplica, and DexYCB-Pt (Sec. 4, pp. 6-9).

7. **2D tracks:** Yes. The paper obtains 2D tracks by projecting inferred 3D tracks onto the image plane and evaluates 2D metrics on LSFOdyssey, DynamicReplica, and TAP-Vid-DAVIS (Sec. 4, p. 6; Table 2, p. 8; Supp. G, p. 17). The trajectory token also includes projected 2D coordinates through the camera projection function `pi_t` (Sec. 3.2, p. 6).

8. **Occlusion / visibility prediction:** Yes. TAPIP3D predicts a visibility trajectory for each query alongside 3D position, supervises visibility with binary cross entropy, and reports occlusion accuracy (Sec. 3 overview, p. 3; Training, p. 6; Evaluation metrics, p. 6). The transformer outputs additive updates for both positions and visibility scores (Sec. 3.2, p. 6).

9. **Multiple views:** Pipeline. TAPIP3D itself is formulated as taking a monocular RGB-D/RGB video sequence with per-frame depth, intrinsics, and optional camera poses, so multi-frame video does not qualify as simultaneous multi-view input under the corrected definition; the paper only shows a supplementary stereo-video pipeline where FoundationStereo first estimates depth from rectified stereo pairs before TAPIP3D tracks in camera/world coordinates (Sec. 3 overview, p. 3; Supp. D, p. 14).
10. **Global context vs. pairwise vs. fixed-window:** Fixed-window. The model operates on fixed-length temporal windows and processes longer videos by advancing a sliding window, initializing the next window from prior estimates (CoTracker3 preliminaries, p. 4; Supp. B, p. 14). World-space coordinates give a common geometric frame, but temporal inference remains windowed rather than globally optimized over all frames at once.

11. **No GT extrinsics required at inference:** Yes. Ground-truth extrinsics are not required at inference because camera poses are optional for camera-space tracking, and for world-space tracking extrinsics may be estimated algorithmically by video 3D reconstruction models or provided by the dataset (Sec. 3 overview, p. 3; Sec. 4, p. 7). The caveat is that TAPIP3D-world still needs some pose source to cancel camera motion and output first-camera/world coordinates (Table 1 caption, p. 7).

12. **Extrinsics as output:** Pipeline. TAPIP3D itself does not estimate camera extrinsics; it consumes optional camera poses from a dataset or an external reconstruction system (Sec. 3 overview, p. 3). The paper composes and evaluates TAPIP3D-world with MegaSaM camera pose estimation, so extrinsics are produced by the evaluated external-geometry pipeline rather than by TAPIP3D's tracking model (Fig. 1, pp. 1-2; Table 1 caption, p. 7).

13. **No GT intrinsics required at inference:** Yes. Ground-truth intrinsics are not strictly required at inference because the paper says camera intrinsics can either be estimated algorithmically by video 3D reconstruction models or provided by the dataset (Sec. 3 overview, p. 3). Intrinsics are still required as an input for lifting and projection, and the authors use ground-truth intrinsics where datasets provide them (Sec. 3 overview, p. 3; Supp. B, p. 14).

14. **Intrinsics as output:** No. TAPIP3D does not output camera intrinsics; it treats intrinsics as an input used to lift 2D features and queries into 3D and to project 3D tracks back to the image plane (Sec. 3 overview, p. 3; Sec. 3.2, p. 6). The paper does not describe or evaluate an intrinsic-estimation head or a TAPIP3D output containing intrinsics (Supp. B, p. 14).

15. **Moving cameras:** Yes. TAPIP3D explicitly targets moving-camera videos by using world-space tracking to cancel camera motion when poses are available, and it reports gains on moving-camera settings such as ADT and LSFOdyssey (Introduction, pp. 1-2; Sec. 4.1, pp. 7-8; Ablations, p. 9). TAPIP3D-camera can also run without poses, but then camera motion remains mixed with scene motion (Sec. 4, p. 7).

16. **Dynamic content:** Yes. TAPIP3D is designed to track dynamic scene points and is evaluated on dynamic or articulated content including LSFOdyssey humanoids, robots, and animals, DynamicReplica humans and animals, and DexYCB manipulation scenes (Sec. 4, pp. 7-8). The introduction frames point tracking as capturing object pose changes, part articulation, deformable structures, and granular materials (Introduction, p. 1).

17. **Feed-forward inference:** Yes. TAPIP3D is presented as a learning-based feed-forward approach, explicitly contrasted with optimization-based dynamic reconstruction methods that require per-video optimization (Related Work, p. 3). The paper reports roughly 10 FPS and 2.6GB VRAM for 1,024 query points over 32 frames on an L40S GPU, with supplementary timing of 11.3 FPS, while noting that external monocular geometry estimation can dominate the full pipeline runtime (Implementation Details, p. 6; Supp. C, p. 14).

**Notes:** TAPIP3D is best understood as feed-forward 3D point tracking over RGB-D or RGB plus externally estimated depth and calibration, not standalone 3D reconstruction (Sec. 3 overview, p. 3). Its performance depends strongly on stable input geometry; the paper notes failures from depth flicker, extreme depth variation, small distant objects, and noisy monocular geometry (Limitations, pp. 9-10). World-space tracking needs camera poses from ground truth or an external method such as MegaSaM, while camera-space tracking can run without poses but includes camera motion in the predicted tracks (Sec. 4, p. 7; Supp. C, p. 14). Training uses Kubric MOVi-F, CoTracker3-initialized image features, fixed 16-frame windows, 3D k-NN/N2N attention, and visibility supervision (Implementation Details, p. 6; Supp. A, pp. 13-14).
