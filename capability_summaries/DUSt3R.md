**Paper:** DUSt3R: Geometric 3D Vision Made Easy, CVPR 2024, DUSt3R
**Summary:** DUSt3R takes RGB image pairs from unconstrained, uncalibrated image collections and predicts dense per-pixel 3D pointmaps plus confidence maps in a single forward pass, without requiring known camera intrinsics or extrinsics. Camera parameters, pixel correspondences, and depth maps are all recoverable from the pointmaps as described in the paper's integrated method; multi-view reconstruction over N>2 images is achieved by running all pairs through the network and globally aligning the resulting pointmaps via a fast gradient-descent optimization (Abstract, p. 1; Sec. 3.1–3.4, pp. 4–6).

1. **3D reconstruction:** Yes. DUSt3R outputs dense 3D pointmaps (X ∈ R^{W×H×3}) that together constitute a point cloud of the observed scene. The global alignment stage (Sec. 3.4) assembles all pairwise pointmaps into a single globally consistent 3D point cloud, and Sec. 4.5 evaluates full 3D reconstructions on the DTU dataset after global alignment (Abstract, p. 1; Sec. 3, p. 3; Sec. 4.5, pp. 8–9).

2. **Dense reconstruction:** Yes. A pointmap is defined as a dense W×H×3 field with a one-to-one mapping between every image pixel and a 3D scene point. The paper explicitly contrasts this with sparse keypoint-based SfM pipelines, and the appendix describes confidence-based filtering only as a visualization post-process (Sec. 3, p. 3: "a dense 2D field of 3D points"; Appendix B, p. 14).

3. **Reference frame:** Selectable. Both pointmaps for a pair are expressed in the first image's coordinate frame; by choosing which image is placed first, any frame can serve as the reference, so both world-space and egocentric output are available depending on input ordering.

4. **Metric scale:** No. The output pointmaps are explicitly stated to be "regressed up to an unknown scale factor" (Sec. 3.1, p. 4). Multi-view depth predictions are described as "only valid up to a scale factor" and are median-normalized for quantitative evaluation; absolute metric scale requires an external reference (Sec. 3.1, p. 4; Sec. 4.4, p. 8).

5. **Point tracking:** Yes. Establishing pixel correspondences between images is a core integrated capability of DUSt3R's method (Sec. 3.3). Correspondences are obtained by nearest-neighbor search in 3D pointmap space (M_{1,2} = {(i,j) | i = NN^{1,2}_1(j) and j = NN^{2,1}_1(i)}), and pixel correspondences are listed as a direct output in Fig. 1 (Sec. 3.3, p. 5; Abstract/Fig. 1, p. 1).

6. **3D tracks:** No. DUSt3R produces pairwise 3D point correspondences but does not output persistent 3D point trajectories over time. No temporal tracking experiment or trajectory output is described (Sec. 3.3–3.4, pp. 5–6).

7. **2D tracks:** Yes. Pairwise 2D pixel correspondences are a direct output of DUSt3R's integrated NN-matching in pointmap space, explicitly listed as "Pixel correspondences" among the method's outputs in Fig. 1. These are pairwise correspondences between any two images, not long temporal tracks; the Auto-Yes rule (Q6=Yes and Q12=Yes) does not apply since Q6=No, but 2D correspondences are a first-class described output (Sec. 3.3, p. 5; Fig. 1, p. 1).

8. **Occlusion / visibility prediction:** No. DUSt3R predicts a per-pixel confidence map C^{v,1} reflecting prediction reliability (e.g., for sky, translucent regions, or hard-to-predict pixels), not explicit occlusion or visibility flags for tracking. Confidence is used to weight the training loss and to filter visualization points, not to label points as occluded vs. visible (Sec. 3.2, p. 5; Appendix B, p. 14).

9. **Multiple views:** No. The core network F always takes exactly two images as input and processes them simultaneously in a pairwise fashion. For N>2 images, DUSt3R constructs a pairwise graph, runs the network on all edges, and then applies global alignment optimization — this is not a simultaneous multi-view forward pass but a pairwise network used within a pipeline. The network itself is fixed to 2 images (Sec. 3.1, p. 4; Sec. 3.4, pp. 5–6).

10. **Global context vs. pairwise vs. fixed-window:** Hybrid. At the neural inference stage, DUSt3R is strictly Pairwise — the network processes one image pair at a time with cross-attention between the two views. For multi-view reconstruction, the global alignment optimization aggregates all pairwise predictions into a joint 3D space, which provides broader scene context but through a separate optimization step rather than learned inference. Multi-view depth also uses confidence-weighted aggregation of multiple rescaled predictions (Sec. 3.1, p. 4; Sec. 3.4, pp. 5–6; Sec. 4.4, p. 8).

11. **No GT extrinsics required at inference:** Yes. A central claim of DUSt3R is operation "without prior information about camera calibration nor viewpoint poses." The network takes only raw RGB images; no camera poses are provided at inference. Sec. 4.5 explicitly emphasizes reconstruction "with no prior knowledge regarding the camera intrinsic and extrinsic parameters" (Abstract, p. 1; Sec. 4.5, p. 8).

12. **Extrinsics as output:** Yes. Camera pose recovery is an integrated component of DUSt3R's own method. Sec. 3.3 derives relative pose via Procrustes alignment of pointmaps, and Sec. 3.4 describes the global alignment optimization that directly estimates all camera poses {P_n} by enforcing a pinhole camera model within the optimization (Eq. 5 and the "Recovering camera parameters" paragraph). Sec. 4.2 benchmarks the resulting multi-view pose estimates. This is DUSt3R's own procedure, not an off-the-shelf external component (Sec. 3.3, p. 5; Sec. 3.4, p. 6; Sec. 4.2, p. 7).

13. **No GT intrinsics required at inference:** Yes. DUSt3R operates without ground-truth intrinsics: the abstract states it requires no camera calibration, Table 3 evaluates multi-view depth without GT intrinsics, and Appendix E tests visual localization with unknown focal length (Abstract, p. 1; Sec. 4.4/Table 3, pp. 8–9; Appendix E, p. 15).

14. **Intrinsics as output:** Yes. Focal length recovery is described as a simple optimization over the pointmap X^{1,1} under centered-principal-point and square-pixel assumptions (Sec. 3.3, p. 5). The global alignment variant estimates full intrinsics {K_n} for all cameras alongside poses and depthmaps. Camera intrinsics are listed as a recoverable output in Fig. 1 (Sec. 3.3, p. 5; Sec. 3.4, p. 6; Fig. 1, p. 1).

15. **Moving cameras:** Yes. DUSt3R is explicitly designed for unconstrained image collections with moving cameras and unknown camera poses. All experiments use images from cameras undergoing substantial motion (CO3Dv2, RealEstate10K, MegaDepth, ETH3D, 7Scenes, Cambridge-Landmarks), and Fig. 8 demonstrates robustness to nearly opposite viewpoints (~180° apart) (Abstract, p. 1; Sec. 4.2, p. 7; Fig. 8, p. 12).

16. **Dynamic content:** No. DUSt3R does not model or evaluate dynamic scene content. Its pointmap representation assumes a single static 3D point per camera ray ("we assume here that each camera ray hits a single 3D point, i.e. ignoring the case of translucent surfaces"), and all training and evaluation datasets consist of static scenes. Dynamic-scene methods appear only in the related-work discussion (Sec. 3, p. 3; Appendix C, p. 14).

17. **Feed-forward inference:** Partial. The core pairwise network F is fully feed-forward (~40 ms per pair on an H100 GPU). However, multi-view reconstruction — the method's primary use case for N>2 images — requires an explicit gradient-descent global alignment optimization over camera poses and scales (Eq. 5, "typically converges after a few hundred steps, requiring mere seconds on a standard GPU"). This is test-time optimization, not a forward pass. Pairwise relative pose via Procrustes is feed-forward; multi-view consistency is not (Sec. 3.4, pp. 5–6; Sec. 3.3, p. 5).

**Notes:** DUSt3R is trained fully supervised on 8.5M image pairs derived from 8 mixed datasets (Habitat, CO3Dv2, ScanNet++, ARKitScenes, MegaDepth, Static Scenes 3D, BlendedMVS, Waymo) with ground-truth pointmaps computed from depth, intrinsics, and poses; it is initialized from CroCo v2 pretraining (Sec. 4, p. 6; Appendix F, pp. 15–17). Scale is always ambiguous without external reference. Intrinsic recovery assumes centered principal point and square pixels. The method handles only static scenes; dynamic objects are not addressed. Input is RGB only (no depth sensor). The pairwise graph for multi-view reconstruction requires image retrieval or exhaustive pairing, and the global alignment optimization scales with the number of image pairs.
