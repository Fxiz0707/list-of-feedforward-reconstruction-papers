**Paper:** Grounding Image Matching in 3D with MASt3R, arXiv June 2024, MASt3R
**Summary:** MASt3R extends DUSt3R for pairwise 3D-grounded image matching. Given two RGB images with unknown camera parameters, a shared ViT encoder and cross-attending transformer decoder predict dense 3D pointmaps, per-pixel confidence maps, and dense local descriptors; a fast reciprocal nearest-neighbor matcher extracts dense pixel correspondences from either the 3D points or the descriptors. The network is trained end-to-end with supervised pointmap regression plus an InfoNCE matching loss, and inference on a single 512-pixel image pair is a single forward pass with no per-scene optimization (Abstract, p. 1; Sec. 3, pp. 3–6).

1. **3D reconstruction:** Yes. The network inherits DUSt3R's pointmap regression heads and predicts two dense 3D point clouds `X^{1,1}` and `X^{2,1}` (H×W×3 each) per image pair. MVS point clouds are also produced as a downstream result by triangulating the dense MASt3R matches; qualitative reconstruction results on DTU are shown in Fig. 5 and Sec. 4.5 (Sec. 3.1, p. 4; Fig. 2, p. 4; Sec. 4.5, p. 9; Appendix A, p. 11).

2. **Dense reconstruction:** Yes. Pointmaps are per-pixel H×W×3 arrays, and the added descriptor head produces per-pixel H×W×d feature maps, giving dense 3D coverage rather than sparse keypoints only (Fig. 2, p. 4; Sec. 3.1–3.2, pp. 4–5).

3. **Reference frame:** Selectable. Both pointmaps for a pair are expressed in the first image's camera coordinate frame; placing any image first makes it the reference, so the world frame is effectively user-selectable via input ordering, providing both world-space and egocentric capability.

4. **Metric scale:** Yes. When ground-truth pointmaps are metric, the paper removes scale normalization from the regression loss (setting the normalizer z = z-hat), training the network to predict pointmaps directly in metric units. The Map-free evaluation confirms metric depth output from MASt3R yields competitive results. This is learned metric prediction conditioned on metric training data, not a domain-invariant guarantee (Sec. 3.1, pp. 4–5; Sec. 4.1, p. 6; Sec. 4.2, pp. 7–8).

5. **Point tracking:** Yes. Establishing dense pixel correspondences between two images is the paper's primary task. MASt3R outputs reciprocal matches between predicted points across the two frames, both via 3D pointmap nearest-neighbor search and via descriptor-based reciprocal matching. These are pairwise correspondences, not temporal tracks, but they do constitute correspondences between predicted points across frames (Fig. 2, p. 4; Sec. 3.2–3.3, pp. 4–5).

6. **3D tracks:** No. MASt3R outputs pairwise pointmaps and correspondences for a single image pair; it does not define persistent 3D point trajectories over a sequence of frames (Sec. 3, pp. 3–6).

7. **2D tracks:** Partial. The auto-Yes rule (If Q6 = Yes and Q12 = Yes) does not apply here. MASt3R does produce dense 2D pixel correspondences between two images via reciprocal matching of local descriptors, but these are pairwise matches without persistent track identities across more than two frames (Sec. 3.2–3.4, pp. 4–6; Fig. 4, p. 8).

8. **Occlusion / visibility prediction:** No. The network predicts per-pixel confidence maps that reflect pointmap regression quality, but the paper does not describe explicit per-point occlusion or visibility flags for unmatched or occluded pixels (Fig. 2, p. 4; Sec. 3.1–3.3, pp. 4–5).

9. **Multiple views:** Yes. MASt3R processes exactly two images simultaneously from two cameras with unknown parameters. Two simultaneous views constitutes multi-view input per the question's framing. It is strictly a fixed two-view model; arbitrary-N multi-view input in a single pass is not supported, and DTU MVS is a pairwise-matching pipeline (Sec. 3, pp. 3–4; Sec. 4.5, p. 9).

10. **Global context vs. pairwise vs. fixed-window:** Pairwise. The network's cross-attending transformer decoder exchanges information between exactly two images; the paper explicitly contrasts this with multi-view global-alignment methods. The coarse-to-fine variant applies independent pairwise calls on overlapping window crops rather than a single global aggregation across many views (Sec. 3.1, pp. 4–5; Sec. 3.4, pp. 5–6; Sec. 4.3, p. 8).

11. **No GT extrinsics required at inference:** Yes. The paper specifies cameras with "unknown parameters"; no ground-truth extrinsics are fed to the model. In the MVS experiments, cameras are used only after matching to triangulate results into the benchmark reference frame (Sec. 3, p. 3; Sec. 4.5, p. 9; Appendix A, p. 11).

12. **Extrinsics as output:** Pipeline. The transformer has no explicit pose or extrinsics regression head. Relative camera poses are recovered downstream by computing essential matrices from MASt3R matches and applying RANSAC, or by PnP on matches (Sec. 4.2, pp. 7–8; Sec. 4.3, p. 8; Sec. 4.4, pp. 8–9).

13. **No GT intrinsics required at inference:** Yes. The formulation assumes cameras with unknown parameters. Relative-pose experiments on CO3Dv2 and RealEstate10K are explicitly run without using ground-truth focal lengths (Sec. 3, p. 3; Sec. 4.3, p. 8; Fig. 1, p. 1).

14. **Intrinsics as output:** Pipeline. There is no explicit intrinsic parameter regression head in the model. The paper notes that focal length can be derived from the predicted 3D geometry as a downstream step, but it is not a direct network output (Fig. 1, p. 1; Sec. 3.1, p. 4).

15. **Moving cameras:** Yes. The entire experimental scope—map-free relocalization, relative pose estimation, visual localization—assumes non-static cameras with strong viewpoint changes, including up to 180° baseline (Sec. 3, p. 3; Sec. 4.2–4.4, pp. 7–9; Fig. 4, p. 8).

16. **Dynamic content:** No. MASt3R assumes pixels correspond to fixed 3D scene points and optimizes for static scene geometry matching; it is not presented as handling moving or non-rigid objects. All reconstruction benchmarks (DTU, Map-free, Aachen) assume static scenes (Sec. 3.2, p. 5; Sec. 4.5, p. 9).

17. **Feed-forward inference:** Partial. The pointmaps, confidence maps, and local descriptors for a 512-pixel image pair are produced by a single forward pass of the trained transformer with no per-scene optimization or test-time training. However, the evaluated workflows layer on iterative reciprocal nearest-neighbor matching (Sec. 3.3), coarse-to-fine repeated MASt3R calls on overlapping window pairs (Sec. 3.4), RANSAC-based pose estimation, triangulation, and geometric consistency post-processing, so the full evaluated pipeline is not a single network forward pass (Sec. 3.1–3.4, pp. 4–6; Sec. 4.5, p. 9; Appendices B–C, pp. 13–17).

**Notes:** MASt3R is fundamentally a 3D-aware pairwise image matcher, not a native arbitrary-N multi-view reconstruction system. Its MVS results come from a pipeline: matches are produced pairwise between each image and all others (one-versus-all), then triangulated; the paper emphasizes that ground-truth camera parameters are not used for matching and are only used afterward to triangulate results into the benchmark reference frame (Sec. 4.5, p. 9; Appendix A, p. 11). Input modality is RGB image pairs at any resolution (coarse-to-fine handles high-resolution images by splitting into 512-pixel overlapping window crops). The model is initialized from the public DUSt3R checkpoint and fine-tuned on a mixture of 14 datasets, 10 of which have metric ground truth, with a combined regression + InfoNCE loss (Sec. 4.1, pp. 6–7; Appendix D, p. 17). Metric scale prediction is conditional on metric training data and may not generalize to unseen domains without metric supervision.
