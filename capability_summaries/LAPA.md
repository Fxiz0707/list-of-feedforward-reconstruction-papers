**Paper:** Look Around and Pay Attention: Multi-camera Point Tracking Reimagined with Transformers, arXiv 2025, LAPA
**Summary:** LAPA takes synchronized video from calibrated multi-camera rigs, uses CoTracker and ViT features per view, and fuses the resulting tracks through geometric/volumetric transformer attention to produce temporally consistent 3D point trajectories. It is trained end-to-end with 3D reconstruction, projection, and attention losses, and performs feed-forward inference for calibrated 2-5 camera setups without per-scene optimization. It does not produce a point cloud or dense 3D representation; its output is sparse 3D trajectories for tracked/query points.

1. **3D reconstruction:** No. LAPA is a point-tracking method rather than a point-cloud or dense 3D reconstruction method: Table 1 marks LAPA's 3D reconstruction capability as unsupported, and the method is defined around tracking M 3D points over time and outputting 3D trajectories (Table 1 and Sec. 3, pp. 3-5).
2. **Dense reconstruction:** No. The method reconstructs sparse tracked/query points only, using CoTracker candidate tracks and an MLP to predict coordinates for those points, not dense scene geometry, surfaces, or point clouds (Sec. 3.1-3.3, pp. 3-5).
3. **World-space output:** Yes. LAPA fuses all calibrated views into a unified 3D volumetric representation and outputs consistent 3D point trajectories in that shared coordinate system, with dataset preparation explicitly enforcing consistent scale and coordinate systems across views (Sec. 3.2-3.3, pp. 4-5; Supp. Sec. 4.4, p. 1).
4. **Camera-space output (egocentric):** No. The paper does not describe native per-camera-coordinate 3D outputs; it outputs unified 3D trajectories and uses camera parameters for projection and geometric attention rather than reporting trajectories in each camera frame (Sec. 3 problem formulation and Sec. 3.2-3.3, pp. 3-5).
5. **Flexible camera frame:** N/A. Since LAPA does not natively output camera-space 3D coordinates, the paper does not provide a flexible choice of output camera frame; any such frame change would be an external transform using known calibration (Sec. 3, p. 3).
6. **Metric scale:** Yes. In the evaluated setups, metric scale is preserved through calibrated, scale-consistent multi-camera datasets, and the model is trained/evaluated against ground-truth 3D points; this depends on supplied calibrated inputs rather than estimating scale from uncalibrated video (Sec. 4.1, p. 5; Supp. Sec. 6.6, p. 6).
7. **Point tracking:** Yes. Point tracking is the core task: LAPA preserves point identities across time and views using cross-view attention and track-query correspondence (Introduction, p. 2; Sec. 3.2-3.3, pp. 4-5).
8. **3D tracks:** Yes. LAPA directly outputs 3D point trajectories and evaluates them with 3D point tracking metrics such as APD and 3D-AJ (Introduction, p. 2; Sec. 3.3, p. 5; Sec. 4.1, p. 5).
9. **2D tracks:** Yes. LAPA consumes per-view 2D tracks from CoTracker and evaluates 2D track consistency by projecting predicted 3D tracks back into camera views; the 2D result is therefore input/derived by projection rather than a separate native LAPA output head (Sec. 3.1 and Sec. 3.4, pp. 3-5; Sec. 4.1, p. 5).
10. **Occlusion / visibility prediction:** No. The method uses CoTracker per-point visibility as input and the benchmark includes occlusion annotations and OA evaluation, but the paper does not define a LAPA output head that predicts per-point visibility or occlusion flags (Sec. 3.1, p. 4; Sec. 4.1, p. 5; Supp. Sec. 6.6, p. 6).
11. **Multiple views:** Yes. LAPA's problem formulation takes synchronized multi-view frames from N calibrated cameras, and experiments evaluate 2-5 simultaneous camera configurations; the supplement states cameras can be added or removed without architectural changes while computation scales with camera count (Sec. 3, p. 3; Table 4, p. 7; Supp. Sec. 6.3/Table S3, pp. 4-5).
12. **Global context vs. pairwise vs. fixed-window:** Hybrid. LAPA aggregates all available camera views into a shared volumetric representation at each step, but its temporal evidence comes through CoTracker windows and momentum-updated track queries rather than a full-sequence global optimizer (Sec. 3.1-3.3, pp. 3-5; Supp. Sec. 4.3, p. 1).
13. **No GT extrinsics required at inference:** No. Known calibration is required at inference: the problem formulation assumes camera intrinsics and extrinsics for each view, and the conclusion lists camera pose estimation for uncalibrated environments as future work (Sec. 3, p. 3; Conclusion, p. 8; Supp. Sec. 6.5, p. 6).
14. **Extrinsics as output:** No. LAPA does not estimate or output camera extrinsics; it consumes extrinsics to project the 3D grid, compute geometric constraints, and form the calibrated multi-view representation (Sec. 3.2, pp. 4-5; Supp. Sec. 4.1, p. 1).
15. **No GT intrinsics required at inference:** No. Known intrinsic matrices are part of the input formulation and projection equations, and the calibration robustness study perturbs those supplied parameters rather than estimating them (Sec. 3, p. 3; Supp. Sec. 6.5, p. 6).
16. **Intrinsics as output:** No. LAPA does not output intrinsics; it uses provided intrinsic matrices for projection, fundamental-matrix computation, and geometry-aware attention masks (Sec. 3.2, p. 4; Supp. Sec. 4.1, p. 1).
17. **Moving cameras:** No. The evaluated setting is a fixed calibrated multi-camera rig with cameras positioned around the scene/world origin; the paper does not claim or demonstrate moving or egocentric cameras (Sec. 4.1, p. 5; Supp. Sec. 6.6/Fig. 2, pp. 6-7).
18. **Dynamic content:** Yes. Dynamic content is central to the experiments and qualitative analysis, including basketball, boxes, juggling, football, robots, complex motions, and occlusions across camera frustums (Sec. 4.1 and Sec. 4.4, pp. 5-7).
19. **Feed-forward inference:** Yes. LAPA is presented as an end-to-end transformer-based feed-forward architecture with real-time FPS measurements and no per-scene optimization loop at inference (Introduction, p. 2; Sec. 4.3, p. 6; Supp. Table S3, p. 5).

**Notes:** LAPA is best treated as calibrated multi-camera sparse 3D point tracking, not dense reconstruction. It depends on CoTracker 2D tracks, DINOv2 ViT features, and known camera intrinsics/extrinsics; calibration noise degrades performance, and self-calibration is explicitly left for future work. The strongest demonstrated configuration is a 3-camera, 16 x 16 x 16 volumetric grid, with diminishing accuracy returns and increasing compute beyond three cameras.
